// 01 — Exam Overview

A quick snapshot of
what you're signing up for.

The IT Officer role is one of the most sought-after specialist posts across India's public sector banks — combining a technical career with banking stability.

Conducting Body
IBPS
Post
IT Officer, Scale I
Mode
Online (CBT)
Language
English / Hindi

An IBPS SO IT Officer works on the technological backbone of public sector banks — maintaining core banking systems, supporting digital infrastructure, leading data security initiatives, and driving fintech adoption. The role blends a stable government-sector career with hands-on technology work, making it a top choice for CS/IT graduates seeking long-term growth.

// 05 — Roadmap

A 12-month plan
that covers everything.

Each phase tells you exactly how deep to go per topic — GATE-level conceptual depth is the sweet spot. Deeper won't hurt. Shallower will. Mocks begin from Month 6 onwards, ramping up as you near the exam.

🎯

The Right Depth — A Quick Reality Check

GATE-level conceptual depth is more than enough for IBPS SO IT Officer. The exam tests breadth over depth: 60 MCQs across 10+ IT subjects in 45 minutes. You don't need LeetCode grinding — no competitive coding is required. The Professional Knowledge paper is 100% theory + conceptual MCQs (e.g. "Which layer does TCP operate at?", "What is 3NF?", "Time complexity of quicksort?"). Use LeetCode only for SQL practice if your DBMS is weak. Focus on understanding + PYQs + MCQ banks — that wins the paper.

🏗️ Month 1 · Weeks 1–4

Foundation — DSA Part 1 + OS Basics

  • DSA (Depth: GATE-level theory): Arrays, Strings, Linked Lists (single/double/circular), Stacks, Queues, Deques. Focus on concepts + MCQs, NOT on solving LeetCode-style coding problems.
  • OS (Depth: Textbook basics): Process vs Thread, Process states, Context switching, basic scheduling (FCFS, SJF, RR). Galvin Ch 1–5 level is enough.
  • Quant: Number System, Simplification, Percentages. 15 problems/day.
  • Reasoning: Syllogisms, Inequalities, Coding-Decoding. 15 problems/day.
  • Completion target: ~8% of total syllabus.

🌲 Month 2 · Weeks 5–8

DSA Part 2 + OS Deep Dive

  • DSA (Depth: Know definitions + time/space complexity): Trees (Binary Tree, BST, AVL, Heaps), Graphs (BFS, DFS — just theory), Hashing basics. You won't be asked to code — just recognize the right algorithm and its complexity.
  • DSA Sorting/Searching: Know Big-O of each (Bubble, Insertion, Merge, Quick, Heap sort + Binary Search). This is heavily tested — memorize the complexity table.
  • OS (Depth: Conceptual + numerical): Deadlocks (Coffman conditions, Banker's Algorithm — practice 5 numericals), Memory management (paging, segmentation), Page replacement (FIFO, LRU, Optimal — numericals).
  • Quant: Ratio, Averages, Profit & Loss, Time & Work.
  • Completion target: ~18% of total syllabus.

🗄️ Month 3 · Weeks 9–12

DBMS — The Highest ROI Subject

  • DBMS (Depth: GATE-level, heavy focus): ER model, Relational algebra basics, SQL (joins, subqueries, aggregate functions — practice on LeetCode SQL problems!), Normalization up to BCNF (must be able to identify NF given FDs).
  • Transactions & ACID: Know each property + concurrency control (locks, 2PL). Very commonly asked.
  • Indexing: B-tree vs B+ tree — conceptual understanding enough.
  • LeetCode SQL tip: Do only the 50 Easy + 20 Medium SQL problems. This is the ONE place LeetCode helps.
  • Continue Quant: Data Interpretation, Time-Speed-Distance.
  • Completion target: ~30% of total syllabus.

🌐 Month 4 · Weeks 13–16

Computer Networks — Memorize the Layers

  • Networks (Depth: Thorough, memorize key facts): OSI + TCP/IP layer-by-layer (know every protocol per layer by heart — this is guaranteed MCQ territory).
  • Protocols: HTTP/HTTPS status codes, FTP (active vs passive), DNS, DHCP, SMTP, POP3, IMAP, TCP vs UDP differences.
  • IP Addressing & Subnetting: Practice at least 40 subnetting numericals — this is scoring gold.
  • Routing: Distance Vector vs Link State (conceptual only, no code).
  • Reasoning: Puzzles, Seating Arrangements, Input-Output.
  • Completion target: ~42% of total syllabus.

⚙️ Month 5 · Weeks 17–20

Software Engg + OOP + COA

  • Software Engg (Depth: Surface-level, memorize): SDLC models (Waterfall, V-model, Spiral, Agile, RAD — pros/cons of each), testing types (Unit, Integration, System, Regression, Alpha, Beta), cohesion vs coupling. This is pure memorization — no derivations.
  • OOP (Depth: Core concepts only): 4 pillars, class vs object, interface vs abstract class, constructor types, method overloading vs overriding. No need to write full Java/C++ programs — conceptual MCQs only.
  • Computer Org & Arch: Number systems (binary/octal/hex conversions — practice!), logic gates, K-maps basics, cache mapping (direct/associative/set), pipelining basics.
  • English: Start dedicated RC practice (2/day), vocabulary drills.
  • Completion target: ~55% of total syllabus.

🔐 Month 6 · Weeks 21–24 · Mocks Begin!

Cyber Security + Cloud + Web Tech + First Mocks

  • Cyber Security (Depth: Definitions + examples): CIA triad, symmetric vs asymmetric encryption (AES, RSA, DES — know key sizes), hashing (MD5, SHA), firewalls (stateful vs stateless), IDS vs IPS, common attacks (SQLi, XSS, CSRF, phishing, MITM), digital signatures.
  • Cloud (Depth: Conceptual only): IaaS/PaaS/SaaS differences (classic MCQ — know one example of each), public vs private vs hybrid cloud, containers vs VMs, hypervisor types.
  • Web Tech (Depth: Surface): HTTP methods (GET/POST/PUT/DELETE), status codes (200, 301, 404, 500), REST principles, cookies vs sessions, basic web security.
  • ⚡ Start mocks this month: 1 sectional mock per week (rotate subjects). Analyze more than you attempt.
  • Completion target: ~70% of total syllabus.

Prelims Sprint (if exam in Month 11)

  • Dedicated Prelims prep: Daily schedule = 1 full Prelims mock + 2 hours analysis + 2 hours Mains revision.
  • Sectional cutoff strategy: You need each section cleared. Identify your weakest section and over-index on it for 2 weeks.
  • Timing drills: Reasoning 50Q in 35 min, Quant 50Q in 35 min, English 25Q in 20 min. Practice this exact split.
  • No new topics this month. Revision only.

🏆 Month 11 · Weeks 41–44 · Prelims Exam Month

Prelims Exam → Instant Mains Pivot

  • Last week before Prelims: Only revision + 1 light mock every 2 days. Sleep well. No new material.
  • Day of Prelims: Reach 90 min early. Trust your prep.
  • Day after Prelims: Immediately pivot to Mains — the gap is only 4–6 weeks.
  • Mains focus: Professional Knowledge is 80% of your score weight. Revise DBMS, CN, OS, DSA hard.
  • ⚡ Mains mocks: 4 per week from this point.
🎓 Month 12 · Weeks 45–48 · Mains + Interview

Mains Exam + Interview Prep

  • Last 2 weeks before Mains: 1 full Mains mock daily. Zero new concepts. Cheat sheets only.
  • Professional Knowledge last-mile: Revise all PYQ Professional Knowledge sections twice. Patterns repeat.
  • Interview prep (starts Day 1 after Mains): HR questions, 2-minute self-intro, banking awareness, 5 tech scenario questions per core subject.
  • Look the part: Formal attire, clean shoes, confident body language. Dress code matters in PSU interviews.
  • Interview weightage: Mains 80% + Interview 20%. A strong Mains score is your buffer.

How Your Basic Pay Grows · Year by Year

The pay scale is structured as ₹48,480–2000/7–₹62,480–2340/2–₹67,160–2680/7–₹85,920. That means: +₹2,000 increment each year for 7 years, then +₹2,340 for 2 years, then +₹2,680 for 7 years — a total of 16 annual increments over the Scale I pay band.

📈

Quarterly DA Revision

Dearness Allowance is revised every 3 months (Feb, May, Aug, Nov) based on the CPI (Consumer Price Index). When inflation rises, your DA — and consequently your in-hand salary — automatically goes up. In recent years, DA has been hovering around 30–40% of basic pay, which is a meaningful quarterly boost of ₹500–₹1,500 on average.

🎯

What Happens After the Next Bipartite Settlement?

Bank officer pay is renegotiated every 5 years through the Bipartite Settlement (between the IBA and bank unions). The 12th Bipartite revised pay w.e.f. Nov 2022, raising basic pay roughly 17–18% and merging a portion of DA into basic. The next revision (13th Bipartite) is due around late 2027. Based on historical trends, expect a further 15–20% hike in basic pay, retrospective arrears covering multiple months, and updated DA/HRA slabs. Real impact: a Scale I officer at ~₹65K in-hand today could cross ₹80K in-hand purely from the settlement — no promotion needed.

How to actually use PYQs

Don't just read them — solve them in timed exam conditions. Use a kitchen timer or an online mock platform. After each paper, spend 2x the paper duration on analysis: what topics you missed, what traps you fell into, and which questions were pure knowledge vs. requiring intuition. Track your attempt count, accuracy, and time per section in a simple spreadsheet — patterns emerge fast, and that's where real improvement comes from.
